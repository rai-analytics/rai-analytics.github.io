Advertising Spend During Peak Inflation: An Analysis of Market Leaders in the Pakistani FMCG Sector (2019–2024)

An independent study examining how 15 of Pakistan's top PSX-listed FMCG companies allocated advertising budgets during one of the country's most severe inflationary periods. I built a Python data-extraction pipeline using AI-assisted parsing (Gemini API) to collect structured financial data from six years of annual reports, constructed a balanced panel dataset, and applied SPSS regression to test long-held assumptions about advertising effectiveness.

Key Finding

Advertising expenditure showed no statistically significant effect on gross profit margin during Pakistan's 2019–2024 inflationary cycle. Firms exhibited "advertising rigidity" — maintaining budgets regardless of macro pressure — a finding that directly challenges conventional FMCG marketing assumptions.

Business Implication

FMCG firms should consider reallocating rigid advertising budgets toward targeted price-promotions and trade marketing during inflationary periods, where measurable ROI is more directly achievable.

A strategic audit of Shan Foods, a leading Pakistani FMCG spice brand, assessing its positioning and exposure during the 2019–2024 inflationary cycle:

Core Strengths & Defense

  • Global Footprint: Spice exports to 65+ countries provided a USD revenue cushion, helping offset domestic currency devaluation.
  • High Brand Equity: Cultural lock-in of recipe mixes (Biryani, Korma) kept domestic demand resilient even as pricing rose.

Category Vulnerabilities

  • Raw Material Shocks: High reliance on imported key ingredients (e.g. black pepper) caused direct gross margin compression.
  • Advertising Rigidity: Continued massive, unmeasured TV/Print advertising investments failed to recover gross margins.

"Recipe for Resilience": Optimizing Rigid Ad Budgets

The Proposal: Homemakers during inflation face budget pressure but want consistent taste. By reallocating 20% of Shan's traditional rigid TV budget into a data-driven digital utility campaign, we drive measurable direct ROI.

1. Smart Portion & Cost Calculator (Digital Web App)
A clean mobile web utility where users input their household size and weekly budget to generate optimized Shan-recipe shopping lists, reducing food waste and proving brand utility.

2. HubSpot CRM Lead Capture & Automation
Drive users to sign up for weekly email meal plans. Automate workflows based on recipes viewed, triggering localized SMS or email discount codes for specific spice SKUs.

3. Closed-Loop Attribution
Supermarket partners scan the CRM-issued discount codes. Shan Foods tracks code redemptions in real-time, calculating exact sales revenue generated per digital advertising rupee.
